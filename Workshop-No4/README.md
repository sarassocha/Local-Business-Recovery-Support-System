## Project Description

This workshop focuses on the simulation and validation of the system designed in the previous workshops by applying computational modeling and experimental analysis techniques.

The proposed system, *Local Business Recovery Support System*, is evaluated through different simulation methodologies to validate system behavior, performance, reliability, and scalability within the Titan Plaza shopping mall environment.

Based on the analysis, design, and project management phases developed in Workshops 01, 02, and 03, this workshop explores operational scenarios, emergent behaviors, and optimization opportunities through simulation and validation processes.

---

## Simulation and Validation Methodology

The development of this workshop follows a structured approach that integrates computational simulation, experimental design, and system validation practices:

---

### 1. System Model Development

The system architecture defined in previous workshops was transformed into a computational simulation model by incorporating:

* Core operational processes such as inventory updates, sales registration, and customer interactions
* Relationships between stores, customers, and system services
* Constraints and quality requirements defined in Workshop 03
* Real operational conditions identified during Workshop 01 analysis

Additionally, simulation parameters were calibrated to reflect realistic business conditions and system behaviors.

---

### 2. Simulation Approaches

Two complementary simulation strategies were implemented:

#### Process-Oriented Simulation

Focused on modeling workflows and operational sequences, including:

* Customer purchase processes
* Inventory synchronization
* Alert and notification generation
* Service response times

This simulation evaluates operational efficiency and process continuity.

#### Behavior-Oriented Simulation

Focused on dynamic interactions and emergent behaviors, including:

* Demand fluctuations and adaptive system responses
* Feedback loops between sales and recommendations
* System behavior during peak commercial activity
* Stability under changing operational conditions

This approach allows the exploration of nonlinear and complex system behavior.

---

### 3. Scenario Design and Experimentation

Several simulation scenarios were designed to test system behavior under different conditions:

* Normal operational scenarios
* High-demand customer traffic scenarios
* Connectivity failure situations
* Inventory shortage conditions
* Optimization and scalability scenarios

Sensitivity analysis was also performed to evaluate how parameter variations affect overall system performance.

---

### 4. Data Integration and Validation

Simulation models were validated using information collected throughout the previous workshops, including:

* Operational observations from Workshop 01
* Architectural decisions from Workshop 02
* Quality and risk considerations from Workshop 03

Validation activities included:

* Comparison between expected and simulated system behavior
* Performance verification under stress conditions
* Reliability and scalability analysis
* Documentation of assumptions and simulation limitations

This process ensures consistency between system design and simulated operational behavior.

---

### 5. Complexity and Performance Analysis

The simulation process allowed the identification of several important behaviors and performance characteristics, including:

* Bottlenecks caused by simultaneous user requests
* Delays generated by synchronization failures
* Emergent patterns during demand fluctuations
* Opportunities for optimization and resource balancing

Performance metrics such as availability, response time, and operational efficiency were analyzed to assess system robustness.

---

### 6. System Evolution

This workshop represents the final stage in the project evolution process:

* Workshop 01: System analysis and problem identification
* Workshop 02: System architecture and design definition
* Workshop 03: Robust system design and project management
* Workshop 04: System simulation and validation

The simulation results validate the feasibility of the proposed system and provide insights for future implementation and optimization.
