### Data Collection Process

**Observation Method**
- 2 observation sessions: Saturday (morning) and Sunday (midday)
- Each session: 1 hour of structured observation
- Variables recorded: foot traffic in small stores areas, crowded zones, customer behavior

**Customer Surveys**
- 20 surveys applied to mall visitors
- 10 surveys on Saturday, 10 on Sunday
- Topics: visit frequency, spending habits, post-pandemic changes, purchase motives

**Employee Surveys**
- 6 surveys applied to employees of small independent stores
- 3 surveys on Saturday, 3 on Sunday
- Topics: customer flow, inventory management, post-pandemic challenges

**Environmental Correlation**
- Weather conditions recorded during each session
- Day comparison: Saturday (morning) vs Sunday (midday)
- Peak hours identified during observations

### Analysis Methodology
- All data was processed using Microsoft Excel
- Comparative analysis between Saturday and Sunday to identify traffic patterns
- Correlation analysis between environmental factors (weather, day, time) and foot traffic
- Four main charts created from survey and observation data:
  - Foot traffic comparison (Saturday vs Sunday)
  - Visit motives distribution
  - Post-pandemic spending behavior
  - Inventory rotation frequency
- Key metrics calculated: average customers per hour, inventory rotation percentages

### Key Findings
1. **Foot Traffic**: Sunday midday shows significantly higher traffic in small stores areas compared to Saturday morning
2. **Visit Motives**: 29% of customers come mainly for other activities like co-working or gym, not specifically for shopping
3. **Weather Impact**: Weather conditions affect customer flow during both sessions
4. **Inventory Challenges**: 20% of small stores report normal inventory rotation, 0% report slow rotation
5. **Post-Pandemic Spending**: 41.18% of customers spend more than before the pandemic
